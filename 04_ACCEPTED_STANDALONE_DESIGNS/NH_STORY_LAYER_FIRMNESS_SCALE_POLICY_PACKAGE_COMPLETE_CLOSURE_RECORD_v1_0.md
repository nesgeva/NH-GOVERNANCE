## 6. What is accepted (current standalone scope)

All policy content physically defined in v1_0 is accepted for the package's
current standalone scope, including:

- **qualitative firmness labels only** — the six-label vocabulary
  (`low_firmness`, `moderate_firmness`, `high_firmness`, `mixed_firmness`,
  `uncertain_firmness`, `omitted_no_support`), Ness's Option B decision,
  with the never-force / less-claiming rule across label boundaries;
- **mandatory evidence basis for every non-omitted firmness value** —
  source-grounded observable signals of the approved kinds, under the
  qualitative evidential-strength hierarchy (direct self-report stronger
  than tone, wording style, or repetition alone; reported speech or
  uncertain attribution lowers strength), recorded in the accepted A2
  `firmness_evidence_basis` slot where the card exists;
- **no numeric scoring, no hidden math, no fake precision** — no number,
  weight, percentage, threshold arithmetic, or scoring function anywhere,
  visible or internal;
- **firmness separated** from truth, confidence, grounding, relevance,
  model confidence, and source reliability — telling-level firmness and
  reading-level confidence under the accepted A2 one-authority rule;
- **provisional/revisable status** — revision only by a new
  reading/telling beside the old through the settled lifecycle, never by
  editing;
- **no direct-inner-state claim** — firmness is an inference from
  observable signals and is never presented as direct access to a person's
  internal state;
- **no forced firmness when unsupported** — conflicting signals produce
  `mixed_firmness`, `uncertain_firmness`, or omission, never a forced
  resolution;
- **A2-consistent omission handling** — `omitted_no_support` is the policy
  name for the no-support outcome; on the accepted A2 card and the built
  v1 embedded entry that outcome remains expressed by honest field
  absence exactly as those contracts fix, with explicit-token
  representation on any other surface deferred to that surface's owning
  schema work.
