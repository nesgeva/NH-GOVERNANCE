## 1. THE SETTLED WONDER BOUNDARY THIS PACKAGE PRESERVES

Master V10 already settles the following, and **nothing in this package weakens, reinterprets, or replaces any of it** (§11 item 30; status table; §0A SMART machinery; §7B Part 6.5; the spine principles):

- **Wonder is isolated simulation/scratch space.**
- **Ordinary real-memory reading paths may not use unselected Wonder scratch material.**
- **Wonder output cannot enter memory directly.**
- **Wonder or simulation material is possibility, not established fact** — it is weightless, dated, confidence-tagged, rejectable, never a fact; N.H wonders forward and keeps every wonder **shown, not decided**.
- **Nothing may silently cross from Wonder into normal memory.**
- **Interpretation, imagination, and model confidence are never authority** — model confidence is metadata, not authority.

The route recorded in §2 exists **alongside** these rules, not instead of them: it is deliberate, selected, and indirect. The "cannot enter memory directly" rule remains fully in force — the A17 route is precisely **not** direct entry.

## 2. THE DELIBERATE ROUTE (Ness decisions 1–2)

- **A deliberate Wonder-to-memory route exists.**
- Ness may deliberately choose **specific Wonder material** and submit that selected material toward normal N.H memory.

Ness is the only selector. The route begins with a deliberate Ness selection and a deliberate Ness submission — nothing else begins it.

---

## 3. WHAT NEVER MOVES, AND WHY NOT (Ness decisions 3–5)

- **Nothing leaves Wonder merely because it was generated, useful, repeated, emotionally important, highly confident, or relevant.** None of those properties is a transfer trigger. Usefulness is not selection. Repetition is not selection. Emotional weight is not selection. Confidence is not selection. Relevance is not selection.
- **Nothing transfers automatically.** Transfer requires a deliberate Ness action — always.
- **Only the material Ness selected may move toward memory.** Unselected surrounding Wonder content — the rest of the scene, session, thread, or scratch space it came from — **remains inside the isolated Wonder space** and remains unusable by ordinary real-memory reading paths.

---

## 4. SELECTION IS NOT ENTRY (Ness decision 6)

**Selection does not create direct memory entry.** The selected material must still pass through the **normal authorized intake, privacy, provenance, and acceptance protections** — the same gates as any other candidate material, unweakened:

- privacy and capture-exclusion precedence (§7Q), applied unchanged;
- identity and authorization (§25; accepted B-INT-5 identity and access wiring), applied unchanged;
- provenance (Bundle 6 — origin and speaker carried from source, never guessed), applied unchanged;
- intake (§7E — the envelope remains the only entry into root stores; the B11 active writable batch only; the sealed 5,521-root batch is never appended to), applied unchanged;
- while Layer 2 remains active, `promote_to_memory()` remains the one authorized path for unverified content into REALITY (§12) — **no direct Wonder-to-REALITY path is created**, and nothing in A17 bypasses Layer 2 or any existing memory-entry protection.

**Which record type a selected item becomes** — a root type, a reading type, a creation-record type, or another technical record — **is not chosen here.** No accepted authority mechanically forces that choice today, so it is left explicitly open for the proper later mechanical package (§9). Whatever record type is later designed, the protections above govern its entry.

---

## 5. PERMANENT WONDER-ORIGIN MEANING (Ness decisions 7–8)

Material originating in Wonder must remain **clearly and durably marked** as:

- **Wonder-originated;**
- **simulation or possibility material;**
- **not an established fact;**
- **not independent evidence that its contents are true.**

**The Wonder origin must not be silently removed** — not because the material later becomes useful, not because it is frequently retrieved, not because it becomes connected to real information, and not because it is accepted into the normal memory system. Acceptance changes where the material lives; it never changes what the material **is**. The origin marking survives acceptance, retrieval, connection, and time.

The exact marking implementation — fields, schema, storage form — is mechanical and open (§9). The **meaning** of the marking is settled here and is permanent.

---

## 6. SURFACING AFTER ACCEPTANCE (Ness decisions 9–11)

Once selected Wonder material has been **properly accepted into memory** through the protections in §4:

- **N.H may bring it up in ordinary use when it is genuinely relevant.** Relevance is judged under the existing relevance rules (§7R governs; relevance is not truth, not evidence strength, not authority, not permanent). Retrieval thresholds and ranking mechanics remain open (§9).
- **N.H must also retrieve or bring it up when Ness explicitly asks for that Wonder material.** An explicit Ness request is sufficient reason to surface it.
- **When N.H brings it up, it must present it as what it is:** an imagined possibility, simulated material, an idea, a theory, a plan, a scene, a rehearsal, or another accurate Wonder-origin category — **never as fact.** The presentation carries the origin, every time, in ordinary use and in explicit-request retrieval alike.

---

## 7. WHAT ACCEPTED WONDER MATERIAL MAY AND MAY NOT DO (Ness decisions 12–13)

**It may** support exploration, comparison, planning, creativity, and recalling what Ness imagined.

**It must not silently serve as:**

- proof of a real event;
- proof of another person's beliefs or actions;
- proof about Ness;
- proof that a prediction is true.

An imagined scene involving a real person is Wonder material about a possibility — it never becomes a fact about that person, and it never enters anyone's story, person-box, or the Living State Web as if it were observed reality. This is the existing never-a-fact law applied, not a new rule.

---

## 8. WONDER ORIGIN AND LATER REAL EVIDENCE (Ness decisions 14–15)

- **If later real-world evidence supports or contradicts the Wonder material, that real evidence must remain separately sourced.** The Wonder item does not become the source of the real-world fact — ever. The real fact stands on its own evidence with its own provenance.
- **N.H may connect the Wonder-origin item to later real evidence only under the existing connection, provenance, and certainty rules** — separate records, separate sources, confidence held honestly, clashes surfaced and never resolved by the system. **It must not merge their identities.** A Wonder item that "came true" remains a Wonder item that is now connected to a separately sourced real record; the two never collapse into one.
