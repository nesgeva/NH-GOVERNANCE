## 7. GRADING STRUCTURE

### 7.1 The six general gold rules — the governing legacy list, preserved

1. `meaning` is judged by **semantic match**, not exact wording.
2. `story_layer` is **not graded** in sealed gold v1 or sealed gold v2-B.
3. **Invention is failure; omission is not failure** when what was stated
   is correct.
4. A **true extra may pass; a made-up extra fails.**
5. A case **may contain multiple acceptable readings.**
6. **Ness alone judges pass/fail; a model may assist but never judges.**

For this new A1 replacement set only, §7.2 adds separate grading of the
Primary Story-Layer result and the Theme result. This does not alter,
reinterpret, or retroactively grade `story_layer` in sealed gold v1 or
v2-B.

### 7.2 The accepted additional A1 structure for this replacement set

**Primary Story-Layer result — judged together as one primary result:**
the telling or tellings; the structured perspective; the evidence
relationship; the firmness label; and the firmness evidence basis.

**Theme result — judged separately** from the primary result.

A case may therefore: **pass both; pass the primary result but miss the
theme; miss the primary result but land near the theme; or fail both.**
The two results are **never combined into a hidden score or a single
undifferentiated verdict.**

**Theme comparison is semantic.** Exact wording is not required, but **a
shallow label fails when it loses an important part of the supported
theme.**

**A gold reference theme does not automatically create or confirm a live
runtime theme record.** It is only the expected benchmark meaning for that
case. Operational theme records, proposal status, and Ness-only
confirmation remain governed separately by the accepted Bundle 3 design
(themes are navigation proposals, never facts; only a Ness response
confirms; repetition and time never confirm).

### 7.3 Evidence-relationship vocabulary (settled)

Every evidence relationship in this file uses exactly one of the five
values allowed by the accepted Story-Layer / A2 contract: **direct
self-report, direct quotation, reported speech, observation, or engine
inference.** No sixth category exists or is created; "direct instruction"
is not an evidence-relationship category. The imperative targets in this
set are recorded as **direct self-report**, per the settled mapping, and
A1-R04's third telling as **observation** — preserved as Ness's
observation and interpretation of other people's conduct, never direct
access to anyone's private inner state. This mapping is mechanically
settled; it is not future open work and it is not a question for Ness.

## 8. MEANING ENGINE RULES THAT GOVERN EVERY CASE

- Use the **whole relevant frozen conversational context**, not only the
  target sentence.
- Keep the **target root distinct** from its surrounding context.
- **Do not import later conversation into an earlier case.**
- **No case is a reread-cycle test.** A1 tests ordinary Engine C
  Story-Layer reading, not CY-F rereading. (A1-R02's target uses the
  everyday word "read again"; it is graded as an ordinary Story-Layer
  reading of that instruction, never as a formal N.H reread-lifecycle
  event.)
- **Preserve the maximum depth genuinely supported by the source.** Never
  flatten meaning merely to shorten a label.
- **Never invent depth, motives, diagnosis, internal states, or causal
  claims.**
- A telling is **local, bounded, from one perspective, at one moment.**
- **Root speaker, subject, and perspective owner remain distinct** roles
  (they may name the same person without being collapsed).
- **Record the evidence relationship for every telling.**
- **Reported or inferred claims about another person are not direct access
  to that person's inner state.**
- **Firmness is how strongly the perspective owner appears to hold the
  stance — never whether the stance is true.**
- **Themes are navigation proposals, never facts.** Zero, one, or several
  themes may appear when genuinely supported.
- **No circular support:** an earlier interpretation cannot prove a new
  telling merely because it was previously stated.
