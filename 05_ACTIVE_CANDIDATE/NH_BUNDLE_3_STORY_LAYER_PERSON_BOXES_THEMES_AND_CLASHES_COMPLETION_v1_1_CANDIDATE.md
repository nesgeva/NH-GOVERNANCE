## 2. PLAIN EVERYDAY MEANING

This file finishes the **design** of Bundle 3 — how N.H keeps organized,
honest records of **people** (Person-Boxes), **recurring threads of
meaning** (themes), and **disagreements in the material** (clashes),
without ever turning organization into truth. In plain words: when a
person is mentioned and it's completely clear who they are, N.H connects
the mention automatically; when it isn't clear, it keeps an honest
"not-sure-yet" anchor; similar names alone never decide anything; two
records that turn out to be definitely the same person may be joined
automatically, and anything less than definite waits as a proposal. A
person's box shows seven separate kinds of material side by side, never
blended into a profile. Themes group tellings without making the grouping
a fact, and only Ness confirms a theme. Clashes are recorded once,
surfaced everywhere they matter, and never resolved by the machine; Ness's
responses are kept as their own events that shape what is shown as
current, never rewriting what was recorded. Nothing here is built yet —
it is a blueprint for ChatGPT to audit and Ness to accept or reject.

## 7. A5 — PERSON-BOX EVIDENCE, LINKING, AND MERGE THRESHOLDS (FINAL POLICY)

The Master left open "exact matching rules, evidence thresholds,
confirmation workflow, and merge mechanics," and provided the slot this
policy now fills: *"Strong source evidence may confirm identity only
under an explicitly authorized resolution rule."* **Ness's decisions in
§6 are that explicitly authorized resolution rule.**

- **The two qualitative tests (no scores, no numbers):**
  - **Completely clear** — the source itself makes it unambiguous who the
    person is (for linking a mention to an existing identity). The
    evidence is in the source material and is recorded as the link's
    evidence basis.
  - **Definitely identifiable as the same person** — the combined
    source-grounded evidence leaves no genuine identity question open
    (for joining two person records/boxes).
- **Automatic action on a met test:** a completely clear mention links
  automatically; when two records are definitely identifiable as the same
  person, **N.H automatically joins them**. **No manual-approval step is
  required or invented for these ordinary clear cases.**
- **Fail-closed on an unmet test:** anything less than completely clear →
  a **pending unresolved identity anchor**, kept unconfirmed; anything
  less than definite → a **pending merge proposal**, never a silent
  merge. Pending items may remain unresolved indefinitely (settled).
- **Similar names or labels alone are never sufficient** — for linking or
  for joining. Name similarity may motivate the mandatory
  search-before-propose step; it never satisfies a test.
- **Evidence basis mandatory:** every automatic link and every automatic
  join records *why* — the source-grounded evidence that met the test —
  alongside the settled link fields (what/why/who-established/certainty/
  timestamp). Certainty is recorded as the applicable test outcome
  (completely clear; pending-unclear; definite; pending-not-definite),
  never a number.
- **Ness's settled options stand unchanged:** confirm, reject, rename,
  keep unresolved, link to an existing Person-Box, or propose a merge —
  available always, required never for the ordinary clear cases.
- **Non-destructive always:** links are never copies; joins never rewrite
  roots, readings, tellings, or historical links; a wrong join is
  corrected through new events or links; history is never rewritten.

## 8. A9 — DOWNSTREAM EFFECTS OF CLASHES (FINAL POLICY, RECOVERED AND NORMALIZED)

An **active clash** changes how material may be *used and shown*
downstream; it never changes the material itself.

- **Recording never blocked:** a clash never prevents readings, tellings,
  links, or responses from being recorded.
- **Surfaced beside, never suppressed:** wherever a clash-involved item
  is consumed — the View Layer, the Person-Box view, the Computed View
  (settled factor 6: active clashes and contrary evidence surfaced beside
  the item), retrieval-labeled context, Living State Web evidence use,
  and action-suggestion support bases — the clash is visible next to it.
- **Conflicted evidence, honestly handled (settled rule only):**
  clash-involved material counts as **conflicted** wherever evidence
  strength matters; under the settled rule, weak, conflicted, stale, or
  insufficient material must either be **withheld or clearly labeled**,
  and must never be silently treated as clean or uncontested support.
  Bundle 2's existing current-support and action-strictness rules
  continue to govern action suggestions; **this Bundle 3 file invents no
  additional action-evidence threshold.**
- **"No clear current view" preserved:** when support is too conflicted,
  the settled §7M capability to say so governs — no forced winner.
- **Ness's responses (by reference, not redesigned):** separate preserved
  events that may affect later presentation and current use — including
  Current/History grouping (§13) and Computed View factor 1 — and that
  never rewrite, erase, merge, or resolve away the underlying material.
- **A clash never closes and is never resolved:** the original clash
  record remains **permanently preserved**. Ness's response is a
  **separate linked event**; it may change later presentation and
  current use. Newer evidence or later detection may append detection
  history, supporting material, or a **proposed refined classification**
  — always through **new linked events**. **None of those things closes,
  resolves, mutates, erases, or selects a winner inside the original
  clash.** The interface may show a response status or a
  current-presentation status, but must never present the clash itself
  as erased or resolved.

## 10. B2 — CLASH RECORDS, DETECTION WIRING, AND DUPLICATE MATCHING

- **Record schema (mechanizing the settled conceptual shape, adding
  nothing substantive):** `clash_id` (stable); clash type (one of the six
  settled types); pointers to the exact readings and roots involved — and
  `telling_id`s where the clash is between tellings (accepted A2);
  description of what specifically conflicts; the retrieval
  configurations and reading modes of the conflicting readings; detection
  mode; detection confidence (metadata, never authority); lifecycle
  state; Ness response status; timestamp. Originals are pointed at,
  never altered.
- **Detection wiring:** both settled §7J detection modes feed **one and
  the same record type** through one commit path: a detection event
  first resolves the **clash identity** — the set of conflicting items
  plus the specific conflicting aspect — against existing clash records.
- **Duplicate matching (settled rule mechanized):** if the identity
  matches an existing clash, **no second record is created**; the
  detection appends a **detection-history event** to the existing record
  (mode, time, configuration, confidence). The same clash never produces
  two records; re-detection adds history, never weight — a clash detected
  twice is not "more real" (§0B no-double-evidence).
- **Genuine-vs-contextual:** the settled core test's outcome is recorded
  on the record (as the type distinction the six types already carry);
  N.H records the distinction and never resolves it.
- **Operation discipline:** one detection event, one append-only log;
  idempotent commit per clash identity (a crashed detection re-resolves
  identity and never double-writes); crash recovery derives state from
  committed records only.

## 11. B3 — THEME SCHEMA, GROUPING, AND TELLING LINKS

- **Theme record:** `theme_id` (stable); label from the Ness-controlled
  open vocabulary; **status `proposed` or `confirmed` — only a Ness
  response confirms**, through B3's own theme-action events below; proposer
  provenance (which engine pass or Ness); creation timestamp; version
  history (append-only; relabeling adds an alias, below).
- **Membership links:** theme↔telling links by `telling_id` (accepted A2
  identity), each carrying the settled link fields (what/why/
  who-established/certainty/timestamp). Membership is non-exclusive — a
  telling may belong to no theme, one, or many. **A membership link is an
  additive organizational assertion pointing back to the original
  telling; it never copies content, never moves it, and never makes the
  grouping a fact.**
- **Grouping:** grouping operations (a theme's member set; theme families
  via aliases) are navigation structures only. **Circular support stays
  forbidden** — pattern-derived themes must retain root support through
  their member tellings' own chains; a theme cannot validate itself by
  frequent proposal; repetition never confirms.
- **Aliases:** theme aliases connect different labels across time
  **without rewriting older tellings or older links** — an alias record
  points both ways; nothing is renamed in place.
- **Theme actions (the Master's settled action set, mechanized — each a
  separate append-only event linked to the stable `theme_id`; the current
  visible theme state is always derived from the preserved event
  history):**
  - **confirm** — happens **only through a Ness response**; the recorded
    confirmation event is the only thing that makes the derived status
    `confirmed`; no repetition, frequency, or passage of time ever does;
  - **reject** — a recorded Ness rejection event; it **never deletes**
    the proposed theme or its supporting membership links; the theme,
    its links, and its history remain preserved, and the derived
    presentation reflects the rejection;
  - **rename** — records a **new label or alias through a new event**
    and **preserves every older label**; nothing is renamed in place;
  - **merge** — records the **relationship** between the source
    `theme_id`s and the resulting **current navigation grouping**,
    **without deleting, copying, or rewriting** the source themes or
    their old membership links; the sources and their full histories
    stand and stay navigable;
  - **split** — records the source theme, the resulting `theme_id`s, and
    the membership-link changes **through new events**, while the
    earlier grouping and its **full history remain preserved**;
  - **leave unresolved** — creates **no** automatic confirmation,
    rejection, aging result, or hidden change; a proposed theme may stay
    proposed indefinitely.

  Across all actions: **repetition and time never confirm anything;
  themes and membership remain navigation only, never facts.** These
  theme response/change events are **part of B3 itself**, not B-AFFIRM.
- **Influence honesty (settled, wired):** if any proposed or confirmed
  theme is ever used to retrieve context for a future engine pass, that
  influence must appear in the retrieval audit trail (accepted B1's
  per-run record carries the why-admitted provenance slot this uses);
  the retrieval-influence *policy* itself remains open (§21). Future
  readings may challenge, omit, or contradict a confirmed theme.

## 12. B4 — PERSON-BOX LINKS, PROPOSALS, AND MERGES (MECHANICS OF §7)

- **Event-record family (all append-only, all carrying the settled link
  fields):** mention-link events (root/reading/telling → box, with the
  §7 evidence basis); pending-anchor creation events (label/name used,
  where it appeared, when, connecting evidence, current test outcome —
  the settled anchor content); anchor-confirmation events; merge
  **proposal** records (the candidate pair, the evidence gathered, the
  unmet-definite gap named); **join events** — **N.H automatically joins
  on a met definite test**; otherwise a join follows only Ness's
  confirmation of a pending proposal — which link the
  identity anchors under **one stable identity view** while every
  constituent record, link, and history remains where it is; **correction
  events** for a join later found wrong — recorded as new events/links
  that re-separate the identity view **without deleting anything**
  (history never rewritten); alias records for names/labels, which attach
  separately from the stable ID and may change.
- **Search-before-propose (settled, mechanized):** before any new anchor
  or proposal is created, the mandatory search runs across confirmed
  boxes, unresolved anchors, aliases, and prior merge proposals — and the
  search's occurrence and scope are recorded on the resulting event.
- **The similar-names guard, mechanical:** name/label similarity is
  marked as motivation-only evidence; it can trigger the search and a
  pending proposal, and can never by itself satisfy §7's tests.
- **Weightlessness:** links, anchors, proposals, and joins organize; they
  add no evidential weight to any reading or telling, promote nothing to
  fact, and never resolve a clash.

## 13. B19 — CURRENT/HISTORY GROUPING AND "CURRENT USABLE" (MECHANICAL DEFINITION)

- **"Current usable," mechanized exactly from the settled §7I words:** a
  reading is **usable** iff it passed the acceptance check AND is not
  marked rejected AND is not marked `insufficient_context`. Usable never
  means true or final.
- **Current grouping:** per root (and per Person-Box section, per theme,
  where those views group), the **newest usable** item is surfaced first;
  revisable, rejected, and insufficient-context items remain visible with
  their clear labels; different reading modes may be grouped separately
  when helpful, with the original chronology always reachable.
- **Conflict surfacing inside Current:** an item involved in an active
  clash carries the conflict beside it ("a conflicting reading also
  exists"), per §8 — Current grouping never hides a clash and never picks
  its winner.
- **History grouping:** strict chronology, complete, always available,
  one switch away. Nothing deleted, hidden permanently, or overwritten.
- **Response events and supersession flags** (Ness responses; Bundle 2's
  newer-evidence-changed/replaced labels) affect **grouping and labels
  only** — presentation-layer inputs that never delete, resolve, or
  rewrite; the underlying preservation and clash records always win over
  grouping convenience.
- **"Current best-supported" delegation (settled):** whenever a surface
  claims *best-supported* or assembles a broader current picture, §7M's
  seven-factor ordering governs; recency stays a limited tie-breaker
  only. B19's grouping never substitutes for it.

## 15. B25 — SEVEN-SECTION PERSON-BOX VIEW MECHANICS (MECHANICS ONLY)

The seven sections themselves are settled and untouched: **(1) roots;
(2) readings; (3) story tellings; (4) clashes; (5) Ness-response events;
(6) themes; (7) unresolved identity anchors and merge proposals.** Each
section states what it contains and its evidential status; sections are
never visually flattened into equivalent claims; order is navigation
only; frequency and recency never silently determine reliability.

Mechanics designed here, inside those walls:
- **Layout:** seven separately headed sections, each opening with its
  settled contents-and-status line; **simple by default** (per-section

- **Filters (the settled set, mechanized):** time, perspective role,

  records**; clearing filters restores the default.
- **Chronological toggle:** one switch to the full chronological view
  across sections, always available.
- **Labels:** every item carries its evidential-status label from its
  owning layer (reading statuses, firmness labels on tellings, theme
  proposed/confirmed, clash type and status, anchor/proposal pending

  displayed, never recomputed by the view.
- **Cross-links:** every displayed item links back to its original
  root/reading/telling/clash/response record; the view holds pointers,
  never copies, and section 4/7 items surface their §16 detail panes.
- **Must-not, mechanical:** no synthesis line, no summary paragraph, no

## 16. B28 — CLASH AND NAMED-GAP PRESENTATION MECHANICS (DESIGN LEVEL)

- **Clash badge:** any surfaced item involved in an active clash carries
  a visible clash marker beside it (§8), in main surfaces and in the
  Person-Box view alike; the marker never reorders, hides, or resolves.
- **Clash detail pane:** opening a clash shows the record's settled
  content — type, the exact conflicting items (navigable pointers), what
  specifically conflicts, detection history, lifecycle and response
  status — and offers the Ness-response affordances, which emit the
  settled **§7J Ness-response events** through B2's commit path, linked to
  any related B-AFFIRM reading event where appropriate (§17) — separate
  records, never collapsed into one. No "resolve" button exists; there is
  only "respond," and responses never erase or resolve the clash.
- **Named gaps:** where an owning layer has **recorded an explicit
  absence** (a gap record naming what is absent and the absence kind as
  that layer defines it), the gap is presented as a labeled absence — a
  named empty slot — never as inferred content, never silently, and
  never as evidence of anything (settled: absence of information is not
  evidence). Gap presentation follows the same simple-by-default /
  complete-on-demand discipline; the gap-kind taxonomy itself belongs to
  the owning layers and is not fixed here.
- **Wording discipline:** main-surface wording stays plain; precise
  grading vocabulary rides in the side-drawer-grade notes per the
  accepted Bundle 2 rules; neither surface overstates certainty.

## 17. B-AFFIRM — WEIGHTLESS READING ACCEPT/REJECT EVENT WIRING (EXACT NARROW SCOPE)

**Scope (from the map, exactly):** B-AFFIRM records the occurrence of
**Ness accepting or rejecting a specific reading** as a **separate,
